Zeitspiegel The delayed dance mirror
Overview Set up at your studio For dancers & students GitHub

Dance now. Watch yourself a few seconds later.

Zeitspiegel is a small appliance — a camera plus a screen — that plays back what just happened in front of it, delayed by however many seconds you choose. Land the move first, then turn around and see whether it actually looked like you thought it did.

What it does

  • Full-screen delayed mirror. Horizontally flipped, like a real mirror. The default delay is 15 seconds and you can change it from 0 up to ~2 minutes on the fly.
  • Adjust from your phone. Connect to the appliance's Wi-Fi, open a web page, drag the slider. No app to install.
  • Save the last clip. Tap a button to download the last n seconds as an MP4 to your phone. Great for sending a take to a teacher or to your own archive.
  • It owns its own Wi-Fi. The appliance hosts an open Wi-Fi network. No venue network needed, no router, no internet.
  • No login, no accounts, no data leaves the box. Plug it in, dance, pull the plug. Frames live in RAM only.

Why a delay helps

When you watch yourself in a normal mirror, you adjust mid-movement — shoulders ease down, knees bend a little more, the line gets cleaner — and what you end up training is the corrected version, not the version you perform without a mirror. With a delayed mirror, you can't correct in real time. You do the move "blind," and then a few seconds later you see exactly what you did. The gap between what you felt and what you see is where the learning happens.

Dancers, climbers, lifters, gymnasts and martial artists have been using delayed-mirror setups for years; they used to require a camcorder, a TV, and a long cable. Zeitspiegel is the same idea as a single, plug-and-play device.

How to use it (the short version)

  1. Plug in power, HDMI, and the USB camera. Wait ~20 seconds.
  2. You see yourself on the screen, delayed.
  3. To change the delay or save a clip, connect to the Wi-Fi network called zeitspiegel on your phone and open zeitspiegel.local.
  4. When you're done, pull the power. Nothing gets saved on the device.

A printable poster with these steps, plus a QR code that joins the Wi-Fi for you, is on the guest page.

Built for studios

Zeitspiegel was made for the way movement studios actually work: lots of different people use the device in a single day, none of them want to log in, and the studio owner doesn't want to debug network problems between classes. So:

  • You can yank the power at any moment without corrupting anything — the storage is read-only by design.
  • It hosts its own Wi-Fi, so it works in a venue with no internet, with bad guest Wi-Fi, or with a router that blocks devices from talking to each other.
  • It costs about €120 in parts plus a screen you probably already own.
